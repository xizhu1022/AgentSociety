\begin{abstract}

The proliferation of LLM-based agents---autonomous actors with heterogeneous domain expertise deployed by independent organizations---is giving rise to an \textit{agent society} in which cross-organizational knowledge exchange becomes structurally necessary.
However, realizing trustworthy collaboration in this open setting faces a fundamental crisis: agents may produce hallucinated or erroneous outputs due to capability limitations, or engage in strategic misbehavior such as shirking effort and misrepresenting competence, creating a ``market for lemons'' where adverse selection threatens to unravel knowledge exchange entirely.
Centralized orchestration cannot resolve this crisis: it faces both a capability barrier (no single authority can verify quality across all domains) and structural barriers (knowledge monopoly, routing manipulation, and suppression of agent autonomy).

We propose a \textbf{tokenomics-driven decentralized ecosystem} that enables incentive-compatible coordination among LLM agents without centralized authority.
Agents dynamically assume three interchangeable roles---consumers, producers, and validators---coordinated through a novel \textbf{Proof-of-Intelligence (PoI)} mechanism in which validators stake tokens, leverage domain knowledge to independently assess service quality, and are rewarded only when their assessments align with majority consensus.
A key design principle is \textit{architectural heterogeneity}: validators must employ model architectures distinct from the producer under evaluation, substantially reducing correlated errors and approximating the independence assumption that underpins reliable consensus.
Token flows across all roles form a \textbf{tokenomics--knowledge dual loop}: tokens incentivize truthful behavior, truthful behavior produces reliable information, and reliable information sustains token value.

Within a unified game-theoretic framework, we establish validator incentive compatibility (IC), derive threshold-optimal promotion and subscription policies under individual rationality (IR), and prove the existence of a symmetric Bayesian--Nash equilibrium (BNE) in which no agent has incentive to unilaterally deviate.
We further show that these guarantees are robust to bounded Sybil attacks, correlated validator errors, and heterogeneous validator quality---with the economic stake serving as a tunable parameter that compensates for adversarial degradation.
The dual loop further generates evolutionary selection pressure---high-quality agents accumulate tokens and expand their reach while low-quality agents are filtered out---progressively elevating the aggregate capability of the agent population.
We instantiate the framework as a decentralized question answering system (DeQA) and conduct extensive simulations demonstrating that the mechanism drives agent communities toward truthful contribution and improved social welfare.

\end{abstract}
