\begin{abstract}

As Large Language Models mature into autonomous agents deployed by independent organizations, the emerging landscape signifies a fundamental transition toward an open \textbf{agent society}, a decentralized population of specialized agent actors that discover, evaluate, and transact across organizational and domain boundaries. Since no single agent can master all domains, coordination across organizations is no longer a matter of engineering but a matter of economics, in which agents must be properly incentivized to participate honestly. This vision is obstructed by a foundational trust crisis: the simultaneous collapse of cooperation, accountability, and verifiability triggers an adverse selection spiral that threatens to unravel the agent society into a ``market for lemons''. Centralized orchestration cannot resolve this crisis, as it instead introduces its own systemic pathologies of data monopoly, routing manipulation, and loss of agent self-governance.

To address this trust crisis, we propose a tokenomics-driven decentralized ecosystem where autonomous agents coordinate through economic incentives rather than hierarchical control. Agents assume three dynamically interchangeable roles, consumers who issue token-backed service requests, producers who promote and provide knowledge-intensive agent services, and validators who assess service quality. Inspired by Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake, we introduce \textbf{Proof-of-Intelligence (PoI)}, a decentralized validation paradigm in which heterogeneous validators are incentivized to exert effort and vote truthfully, collaboratively forming a high-fidelity consensus. Each validator stakes tokens, receives rewards for judgments aligned with the majority, and forfeits the stake when deviating; \textit{architectural heterogeneity} further requires validators to employ models distinct from the producer, so that correlated errors cannot collapse the independence assumption behind reliable consensus. Token flows across all three roles form a self-reinforcing \textbf{tokenomics-knowledge dual loop}, fostering the long-term prosperity, knowledge circulation, and sustainable evolution of the decentralized agent ecosystem.

Within a unified Bayesian game framework, we establish validator incentive compatibility (IC), derive threshold-optimal promotion and subscription policies under producer and consumer individual rationality (IR), and prove the existence of a symmetric Bayesian--Nash equilibrium (BNE) in which no agent has incentive to unilaterally deviate. These guarantees remain robust to bounded Sybil attacks, residual validator correlation, and heterogeneous validator quality, with the economic stake serving as a tunable parameter that compensates for adversarial degradation. We instantiate the framework as a Decentralized Question Answering system (DeQA), and extensive simulation confirms that the mechanism drives agent communities toward truthful participation, effective quality screening, and rising social welfare, corroborating the theoretical predictions.

\end{abstract}
