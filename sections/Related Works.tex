\textbf{Decentralized Systems.}
Decentralized systems have redefined digital coordination by enabling trustless value exchange and computation without central authority. From peer-to-peer sharing to blockchain-based smart contracts, infrastructures like Ethereum \cite{buterin2014ethereum} introduced programmable logic and token incentives to power autonomous applications.
To enhance scalability and modularity, recent architectures adopt rollups and data availability layers \cite{celestia2025data}, while decentralized storage ensures verifiable and persistent data access \cite{trautwein2022ipfs}.
Since most platforms still rely on centralized pipelines, lacking transparency, incentive alignment, and robustness to adversarial behaviors \cite{min2025llm}.
To address this gap, we propose a present decentralized QA framework with token-incentivized query-responder routing and validator-mediated interaction. Our  mechanism enables transparent, auditable, and self-organized knowledge exchange beyond centralized control \cite{dsqa2025}.


\noindent \textbf{Economic Incentives and Tokenomics.}
Economic incentives are central to the viability of decentralized systems. Unlike Web2 platforms with centralized control, Web3 protocols embed economic coordination into their infrastructure via programmable tokens, a paradigm known as \textit{tokenomics}, which governs token issuance, distribution, and utility across roles \cite{ito2024cryptoeconomics,abadi2024governance,zuo2023set}.
Recent systems apply tokenomics to sustain participation and core functionality: staking secures consensus \cite{cong2025tokenomics}, liquidity mining supports decentralized exchanges \cite{augustin2024yield,liu2022two-swaps}, and governance tokens incentivize protocol stewardship \cite{abadi2024governance}. These mechanisms enable trustless coordination and help bootstrap growth, security, and decision-making.
Yet, existing designs largely target financial systems. We extend tokenomics to decentralized ecosystem, where tokens not only regulate access but also incentivize retrieval, validation, and participation \cite{allen2023airdrops}.
