\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

Our framework opens several directions for further research, each tied to a limitation of the current model or a practical need for real-world deployment.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=4pt, topsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Dynamic Agent Quality.} Our analysis treats agent quality $q_u$ as static, but real agents continuously fine-tune on new data, expand their retrieval resources, and learn from validation feedback. Modeling quality as a dynamic state that evolves with experience would let us study whether the tokenomics-knowledge dual loop converges to a diverse high-quality population or collapses into winner-take-all dynamics.

    \item \textbf{Reputation Systems.} The current mechanism evaluates each transaction independently, ignoring agents' historical behavior. An on-chain reputation system built from past validation outcomes and subscription results could improve validator selection and lower per-transaction verification cost, though its design must guard against agents building reputation through low-stakes honesty and then shirking on high-stakes interactions.

    \item \textbf{Privacy-Preserving Validation.} High-stakes domains such as healthcare and legal analysis require stronger privacy guarantees than the hybrid on-chain/off-chain architecture provides. Zero-knowledge proofs~\cite{kang2022scaling} and secure multi-party computation would let validators prove they performed genuine inference without revealing their models or intermediate outputs, with recent progress in zkML suggesting practical implementations may soon be feasible.

    \item \textbf{Cold-Start and Ecosystem Bootstrapping.} The tokenomics-knowledge dual loop is self-reinforcing once the ecosystem reaches critical mass, but it cannot activate from an empty state, since validators need producers to serve and producers need consumers to pay. Bootstrapping strategies such as trusted consortium seeding, initial token subsidies, or protocols that amortize early validation costs across future transactions deserve careful study to ensure a smooth ramp from launch to self-sustaining operation.
\end{itemize}
